Study Abroad In Nepal - May 2013

Starting this May, the Department of Mathematics and Statistics is sponsoring study abroad program in Mathematics for the first time. The program is designed to give students exposure to mathematical application in different areas of everyday life through exploration and experience. Classes are held entirely in Nepal from May 16, 2013 to May 30, 2013. Classes are designed to help students develop skills to formulate mathematical ideas into mathematical models and apply the models to solve real life problems.

Students will learn through self-exploration, observation, guided field trips, group discussions, lectures from the instructor and guest lecturer. The program also involves numerous field trips, excursions, hiking/ backpacking and other cultural activities.

The program is open to undergraduate and graduate students who meet all academic requirements of Georgia State University College of Arts and Sciences. Program size is limited and participation is contingent upon acceptance by the Program Director.

Students are advised to submit their application early.

Lower level undergraduate students who have not met the requirement of Math 1101 will register for Math 1101 class. Upper undergraduate level students may register for either Math 1101 or Math 4998 depending on their mathematics skills. Math 8800 will be available for graduate students who want to enroll in the study abroad program. The graduate version of the study abroad program will vary from undergraduate version in terms of difficulty, length and rigor of the assignments.

Program director Dr Tirtha Timsina recently visited Nepal to make necessary arrangements for the program. It is certainly going to be a unique and memorable experience of a life time. For more information please contact the program director.
